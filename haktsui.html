Crab Cave at Hak Tsui

In addition to Longji, the Shek O hiking route also has Hezui. Hok Tsui, located at the southern tip of Hong Kong, is a statutory coastal protection area. Visitors need to follow the park rules and take care of the natural environment of Hong Kong. The route will pass through Hezui Village, the launching station, and whale bone specimens, and then come to two sea caves, Leiyin Cave and Crab Cave, which are naturally washed by sea water. The crab-shaped entrance of the cave attracts many visitors to check in. On holidays, you may need to line up to take pictures of the entrance of the cave. When taking pictures, you should also be careful to be baptized by sea water.


Hok Tsui check-in route details
Route: Hezui Road -> Hexui Village Genglou -> Telecom’s launch station -> Hexui Lighthouse -> Leiyin Cave -> Whale bone specimen -> Crab cave (check-in point) -> Hexui Road
Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆
Time: about 5 hours
